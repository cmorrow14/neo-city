Reimagining the Future of Coastal Cities

NeoCity Vision Studio designs sustainable, culturally grounded urban districts — starting with Zanzibar.

Our Vision

At NeoCity Vision Studio, we build cities that create jobs, housing, and clean infrastructure without selling off land or importing foreign sprawl. By leasing land instead of selling, we ensure governments retain control and local communities benefit directly. Our work is rooted in cultural respect and environmental stewardship—because the future belongs to those who design it thoughtfully.

Grounded Approach

No hype, no buzzwords. Just real cities for real futures, designed with and for local communities.

Environmental Stewardship

Sustainable infrastructure, clean energy, and waste management that respects local ecosystems and heritage.

Cultural Respect

Design that honors local traditions, Islamic heritage, and community identity—ensuring every detail reflects place.

Why Now?

Climate pressures, shifting tourism patterns, and increasing migration demand a new urban model—one that respects culture, fosters local prosperity, and protects the environment. NeoCity is that model.

Growing Tourism Economy

Tourism is expanding rapidly, creating demand for sustainable development models.

Community-Driven Growth

Local communities need urban models that preserve land and culture—now more than ever.

Environmental Resilience

Sea-level rise and resource scarcity demand proactive, sustainable planning today.

The Project: Kisiwa Mpya

Kisiwa Mpya—“New Island”—is our 1-square-mile prototype in Zanzibar. Built around clean infrastructure, tourism, and dignity, it keeps land public and centers community benefit. It’s a living blueprint for future coastal cities.

How It Works

99-Year Ground Lease

NeoCity asks for a 99-year ground lease—land remains in public hands, funding infrastructure and local programs for generations.

Culturally Integrated Architecture

Designs that honor Zanzibar’s Islamic heritage and local traditions, creating spaces for community and commerce.

Walkable, Human-Scale Design

Compact streets and community paths prioritize pedestrians, fostering social connection and accessibility.

Community-Centered Welcome Center

An orientation hub for newcomers, featuring interactive exhibits that share local history and culture.

Youth Maritime & Cultural Programs

Sailing and kayaking training, plus heritage workshops—empowering the next generation of local leaders.

Sustainable Infrastructure

Clean energy, water management, and waste systems designed for resilience and minimal environmental impact.
